# Skills & competencies

Use this as a **self-assessment map** for **Staff QE**, **Quality Manager**, and **QA Director** readiness—not a linear checklist. Senior craft (below) is table stakes; later rows add what usually differentiates at each level.

## Foundation (still expected at Staff / many EM roles)

- **Testing craft** — risk-based design, exploratory methods, clear defect communication, automation judgment (what not to automate).
- **Technical depth** — enough to read/debug code in your stack, API and contract thinking, CI/CD and environments, basic observability. *Practice notes* (e.g. [integration testing](practices/integration-testing.md)) live under [`practices/`](practices/) when you want depth beyond this overview.
- **Collaboration** — working with product and engineering on acceptance criteria, trade-offs, and release readiness without being a gatekeeper.

## Staff Quality Engineer — additional emphasis

- **Systems thinking** — end-to-end flows, test data strategy, env parity, flake and stability as an engineering problem.
- **Strategy** — quality goals tied to business/customer risk; written guidance others actually adopt.
- **Test architecture** — patterns for large suites, ownership boundaries, platform vs product test code, vendor fit.
- **Technical influence** — reviews, RFCs, pairing with senior ICs outside QE; building trust with Staff+ peers.
- **Organizational learning** — postmortems, blameless culture, turning incidents into durable prevention.

## Quality Manager — additional emphasis

- **People leadership** — hiring, performance cycles, growth plans, inclusion, and hard feedback.
- **Org and process** — how QEs align to squads/tribes; escalation paths; quality rituals that scale.
- **Stakeholder communication** — exec-ready narratives; honest metrics; cost-of-delay and cost-of-quality framing.
- **Operational ownership** — sometimes budget, vendors, contractors, and cross-functional programs.
- **Judgment under pressure** — shipping vs. holding the line; when to absorb risk vs. escalate.

## QA Director — additional emphasis

- **Multi-level org leadership** — clarity of roles across managers and Staff ICs; **scaling** the function without centralizing every decision on you.
- **Strategy and portfolio** — multi-year quality and test platform roadmaps aligned with **company** bets, not only one product line.
- **Executive and external presence** — credible with **VP+** peers; sometimes customers, auditors, or partners.
- **Economic judgment** — total cost of quality initiatives; **ROI** framing for platforms and headcount.
- **Calm under systemic failure** — large incidents, regulatory pressure, or reputational risk; **process without paralysis**.

## Resources to anchor vocabulary

- **James Bach / Michael Bolton** — context-driven testing, exploratory testing (search: Satisfice, RST).
- **Cem Kaner** — resisting shallow “manual vs automated” framing.
