# Learning paths

Growth toward **Staff QE**, **Quality Manager**, and **QA Director** usually assumes a solid senior foundation (testing craft, automation, CI, stakeholder communication). Below is a **leadership-oriented** map; reorder based on your gaps.

## Staff Quality Engineer (IC)

1. **Cross-team systems** — test data, environments, shared pipelines, flakiness and observability at org scale.
2. **Strategy in writing** — quality goals tied to product risk; testability requirements; definitions of “good enough” for release.
3. **Technical leadership** — architecture reviews for test code, platform decisions, vendor/tool evaluation, TCO.
4. **Influence** — working with Staff+ peers in product and engineering; resolving disagreements on risk with evidence.
5. **External craft** — conferences, internal guilds, or open standards that sharpen how the org thinks about quality.

## Quality Manager

1. **People leadership fundamentals** — feedback, coaching, difficult conversations, delegation, inclusion.
2. **Org design** — how QEs embed (central vs embedded), career ladders for ICs, ratios and capacity models.
3. **Operating cadence** — hiring loops, quality reviews with leadership, incident/postmortem rituals that stick.
4. **Business fluency** — roadmap trade-offs, cost of quality, vendor contracts, sometimes budget and forecasting.
5. **Stakeholder narrative** — metrics that are honest (not vanity), executive summaries, aligning quality work to outcomes.

## QA Director

1. **Org-scale leadership** — developing **senior managers and senior IC leads**, succession, and a coherent **leveling and promotion** story for QE.
2. **Executive communication** — board- or C-level narratives where relevant; **annual planning**, cross-portfolio prioritization, and **trade-offs** between quality investment and business timelines.
3. **Financial stewardship** — OPEX/CAPEX for tools and platforms, vendor consolidation, **build vs. buy**, contractor strategy.
4. **Cross-functional systems** — how quality interfaces with **GRC, security, SRE, support, and product**; escalation and **crisis** playbooks at company scale.
5. **Industry and customer lens** — competitive quality, SLAs, regulated markets—enough to represent the function **outside** engineering when needed.

## How to structure your own plan

- Pick **one primary theme** per quarter (e.g., “written quality strategy for my domain” or “hiring loop for senior QE”).
- Tie learning to **visible outcomes**: a adopted standard, a staffed initiative, or a leadership artifact (doc, review, talk).
- For Staff ICs, keep a **small hands-on anchor** (e.g., one critical framework area) so skills stay credible.
